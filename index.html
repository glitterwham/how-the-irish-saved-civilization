Description

The perfect gift of St. Patrick’s Day, and a book in the best tradition of common history — the untold story of the role of Ireland in sustaining Western civilization as the Dark Ages descended on Europe.

Millions of Americans celebrate St. Patrick’s Day every year but may not be aware of how important a St. Patrick’s impact was on the subsequent development of humanity. He not only brought Christianity to Ireland but also instilled a sense of literacy and learning that would establish the conditions that allowed Ireland to become “the island of saints and scholars” — and thus maintain Western civilization while Europe was being invaded by barbarians.

Thomas Cahill tells the tale in this exciting and convincing account about how Europe progressed from Rome’s golden period into the medieval era. The transfer could not have happened without Ireland. Not only did Irish monks and scribes retain the very record of Western history — copying Greek and Latin writers ‘writings, both pagan and Christian, while on the continent libraries and learning were forever lost — they introduced to the mission their peculiar Irish world-view.

As Cahill shows delightfully, so much of the vibrancy that we associate with medieval culture has its origins in Ireland. In the European continent, when the seeds of civilization were replanted, it was from Ireland that they germinated.

In the spirit of A Distant Mirror by Barbara Tuchman, How The Irish Saved Humanity reconstructs a period that few know about but that is central to understanding our history and cultural heritage. Yet it conveys its experience with a winking wit, reflecting aptly the resilience of the unsung Irish who has relaunched civilization.
